  • Special attention to kidney issues that arise during cancer treatment.

Medical Oncology Track

Cancer care led by Dr. S. Sivayogini

The oncology pathway combines clinical accuracy, multidisciplinary coordination, systemic treatment planning, supportive symptom management, survivorship review, and palliative support when care goals shift.

  • Cancer evaluation, pathology interpretation, imaging review, and staging.
  • Therapy planning across chemotherapy, targeted therapy, hormonal therapy, and immunotherapy.
  • Strong emphasis on counselling, comfort, and family communication at every step.

Shared care matters most when cancer treatment affects kidney health.

Onco-nephrology is a defining strength of the clinic. Patients can receive kidney-focused support for chemotherapy-related injury, fluid and electrolyte concerns, and renal issues that complicate ongoing cancer treatment.

  • Coordinated decision-making across oncology and nephrology.
  • Better continuity for medically complex patients.
  • Supportive and palliative care included as core components of care.
Evidence-based review Clinical decisions anchored in diagnosis, reports, imaging, and response monitoring.
